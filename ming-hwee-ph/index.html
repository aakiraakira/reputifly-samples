Frequently Asked Questions

Everything applicants, employers, and families want to know before committing.

What documents do I need to apply?

You will need a valid Philippine passport with at least 6 months validity, a recent NBI Clearance, an updated resume, 2x2 and whole-body photos, and copies of your skills certifications (TESDA, diplomas, training certificates). Additional documents may be required depending on your destination country and job category.

Are there processing fees and what do they cover?

Ming Hwee follows all DMW fee guidelines. Job seekers pay only government-regulated fees for documentation, medical exams, training, and deployment. We provide a transparent breakdown of every peso before you sign any contract, and we never charge illegal placement fees.

What training is provided before deployment?

All deployed workers receive mandatory Pre-Departure Orientation Seminar (PDOS) plus role-specific training such as TESDA-accredited Household Services, caregiving, elderly care, and cooking modules. Skilled workers receive destination-specific briefings on workplace culture, rights, and emergency contacts.

How long from application to deployment?

Typical timelines run from 6 to 12 weeks depending on the destination country, employer match speed, and document processing. Hong Kong placements are usually fastest at 6-8 weeks, while Dubai and Singapore may take 8-12 weeks due to visa requirements.

Which countries can I work in?

Ming Hwee actively deploys to Hong Kong, Singapore, Malaysia, and Dubai (United Arab Emirates). We match domestic helpers and skilled workers (caregivers, construction, hospitality, F&B, manufacturing) to vetted employers under DMW-approved contracts.

What are my contract rights?

Every contract we process adheres to DMW standards: minimum wage guarantees, weekly rest days, paid annual leave, employer-paid return airfare, medical insurance, and repatriation rights. You will review and sign the contract only after understanding every clause, with a Ming Hwee counselor present.

What if I have a problem with my employer abroad?

Ming Hwee provides post-arrival welfare support throughout your contract. Call or message our welfare desk anytime and we coordinate with the destination partner agency, POLO office, or Philippine Embassy to resolve disputes, medical issues, or repatriation needs.

Where is the nearest Ming Hwee office?

We have three Philippine offices: Manila Headquarters at JKK Pacific Building, Malate; Iloilo City at E. Villanueva International Bldg; and Davao City at MGR Building II, Matina Crossing. Walk-ins are welcome during business hours, or apply online and we will contact you within 24 hours.
