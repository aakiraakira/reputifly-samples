The CDI Approach to MBTI®

Unlike standard assessments, CDI facilitates an interactive exploration of the four dichotomies. We focus on how these preferences manifest in a high-pressure corporate environment:

  • Energy Source (Extraversion vs. Introversion): We explore how team members recharge and contribute in meetings — ensuring that the "quiet brilliance" of introverts is leveraged alongside extroverted energy.
  • Information Intake (Sensing vs. Intuition): We bridge the gap between "detail-oriented" executors and "big-picture" visionaries to prevent communication silos.
  • Decision-Making (Thinking vs. Feeling): We teach leaders how to balance objective logic with the "human impact," fostering a culture of Psychological Safety.
  • Lifestyle Structure (Judging vs. Perceiving): We provide strategies for managing different approaches to deadlines and spontaneity, reducing workplace friction.

Commonly Asked Questions

Key Information for Your MBTI® Workshop

What is the MBTI® and how does it work?

The MBTI® is a psychometric tool designed to measure psychological preferences in how people perceive the world and make decisions. It identifies 16 distinct personality types based on four scales: Extraversion/Introversion, Sensing/Intuition, Thinking/Feeling, and Judging/Perceiving. During the workshop, our experts help participants understand these preferences to improve self-awareness and team dynamics.

What's the difference between a half-day and full-day session?

A half-day session (4 hours) focuses on "Self-Discovery," covering individual profile debriefs and basic communication strategies. A full-day session (8 hours) allows for "Team Integration," adding advanced modules such as Conflict Management, Change Management, and intensive Team Mapping exercises to apply the data to real-world workplace challenges.

What is the typical group size?

To ensure a high-impact experience with personalized feedback, we typically recommend 10 to 50 participants per session. However, the format is highly scalable; our experts can adjust the delivery to suit intimate leadership teams or larger departmental groups depending on your specific needs.

Can we conduct the workshop at our office?

Yes. We are fully equipped to deliver onsite training at your location to make the process as seamless as possible for your team. We provide all necessary workshop materials, and we only require a suitable meeting space with audio-visual capabilities (projector/screen) to facilitate the session.

Can this workshop help with specific team conflicts?

Absolutely. MBTI® is one of the most effective tools for conflict resolution because it depersonalizes disagreements. By understanding that a colleague isn't being "difficult" but is simply processing information differently, teams can move from frustration to constructive problem-solving.
