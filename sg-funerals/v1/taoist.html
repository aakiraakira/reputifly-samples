What is a Taoist Funeral?

A spiritual ceremony rooted in beliefs about the afterlife, balance (Yin and Yang), and ancestral respect.

Body Preparation

Cleansing the body is a vital first step, ensuring a peaceful departure and symbolizing the start of the spiritual journey into the afterlife.

Altar Offerings

Food, incense, and joss paper are placed at the altar. These offerings support the deceased and show filial piety from the descendants.

Chanting & Rites

Led by Taoist priests, chanting communicates with the spiritual realm, seeking forgiveness and paving the way for the soul's safe passage.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a Taoist funeral last?
+
Taoist funerals typically last for an odd number of days—usually 3, 5, or 7 days. The duration often depends on the family's wishes and the availability of cremation slots.
Why is rice placed on the coffin (Hokkien tradition)?
+
For Hokkien Taoists, a bag of rice is often placed on the coffin. Money collected from family members is placed inside. After the funeral, the rice is returned to the family to be cooked and eaten, symbolizing blessings and good luck for future generations.
What happens to the paper offerings?
+
Paper offerings (houses, cars, money, mountains) are burnt during the final night or on funeral day. This act transfers these material goods to the spirit world, ensuring the deceased lives comfortably in the afterlife.
Do I need to know my dialect group?
+
It is helpful, as rites differ slightly. However, if you are unsure, our experienced funeral directors can guide you based on your family name or simple questions to ensure the correct traditions are observed.
What should family members wear?
+
Family members typically wear white t-shirts and black or dark blue pants. We provide mourning badges to be worn on the sleeve. Guests should generally wear sombre colors (white, black, blue) and avoid red.
Are payment plans available?
+
We practice transparent billing. A deposit is usually required upon confirmation, with the balance due after the funeral. We accept PayNow, Bank Transfer, and Cheques. Please speak to our director for specific arrangements.
Do you handle the location booking?
+
Yes, our package includes the coordination and booking of the HDB Void Deck or Funeral Parlour, as well as the cremation slot booking at Mandai or other crematoriums.
