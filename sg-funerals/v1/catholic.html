Catholic Funeral Rites

The funeral liturgy is a time to offer worship, praise, and thanksgiving to God for the gift of life.

Vigil & Night Prayers

Family and friends gather to pray for God's mercy. Prayers are led to comfort the living and plead for the forgiveness of the departed's sins.

The Funeral Mass

Final Commendation

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens during a Catholic Funeral Mass?
+
The Funeral Mass typically occurs on the final day before cremation/burial. It involves the reception of the body, placing of the Pall and Bible, Liturgy of the Word, Holy Communion, and the Final Commendation where incense and holy water are used to bless the deceased.
What should family members wear?
+
There is no strict rule, but white or dark-colored attire is respectful and traditional for Catholic funerals in Singapore. It is a solemn event, so modest dressing is recommended.
Can non-Catholic family members participate?
+
Absolutely. Non-Catholic friends and family are welcome to attend the wake, prayers, and Mass. They may participate in all rites except for receiving Holy Communion, which is reserved for practicing Catholics.
Do you provide a priest if we don't have one?
+
Yes. If you do not belong to a specific parish or need assistance, we have a Catholic Funeral Minister who can conduct the service, or we can assist in contacting a parish on your behalf.
Are payment plans available?
+
We practice transparent billing. A deposit is usually required upon confirmation, with the balance due after the funeral. We accept PayNow, Bank Transfer, and Cheques.
