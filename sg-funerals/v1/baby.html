Supporting You Through Loss

We understand that the loss of a child is indescribable. There is no "right" way to grieve.

Important Regulations

If a baby passes before 22 weeks and no Birth Certificate was issued, a doctor's memo authorizing cremation is required. We assist in verifying if an official Death Certificate is needed.

A Personal Farewell

Parents often place personal belongings, toys, or hand-written letters into the coffin. Writing letters can be a therapeutic way for mothers and fathers to express their love.

Handling Ashes

We can arrange for ash dispersal at sea, interment at a columbarium, or help you choose functional keepsake urns to keep a portion of the ashes at home.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a death certificate?
+
If the baby is over 22 weeks, a Certificate of Stillbirth or Death Certificate is usually issued. For under 22 weeks, a doctor's memo authorizing cremation is required. We can guide you through obtaining these documents.
Can we hold a wake?
+
Yes, parents can choose to hold a private memorial or wake. However, many choose direct cremation for infants. The choice is entirely up to you and your spouse.
Is there much ash after cremation?
+
For small babies, the amount of ash collected is very little. We handle this delicate process with extreme care to ensure whatever remains are collected for the parents.
Are payment plans available?
+
We practice transparent billing. A deposit is usually required upon confirmation, with the balance due after the funeral. We accept PayNow, Bank Transfer, and Cheques.
