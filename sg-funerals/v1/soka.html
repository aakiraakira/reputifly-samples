What is a Soka Funeral?

A ceremony focusing on the chanting of Nam-myoho-renge-kyo to guide the deceased towards enlightenment.

Community Support

Unlike other traditions, SGI does not have priests. We coordinate with Soka leaders and the tight-knitted community to lead the rites.

Power of Chanting

The chanting of Nam-myoho-renge-kyo is central to the service, intended to guide the deceased towards enlightenment (Eagle Peak).

Law of Causality

Reflecting the teachings of Nichiren Daishonin, we honor the belief in eternal life and the potential to turn "evil into goodness" through faith.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there priests at a Soka Funeral?
+
No. SGI does not have priests or temples. Instead, leaders and community members from the Soka Association conduct the service and lead the chanting of Nam-myoho-renge-kyo.
What is the significance of the chanting?
+
It is believed that chanting activates the state of Buddhahood in the chanters' lives. The collective chanting borrows upon the power of the Mystic Law to guide the deceased towards enlightenment (Eagle Peak).
Can non-Soka members attend?
+
Yes, absolutely. The Soka community is welcoming. While the chanting is central to the rites, friends and family of all faiths are welcome to pay their respects and offer condolences.
Do you help coordinate with the Soka Association?
+
Yes. We work closely with the Soka Association to ensure all items needed are provided and that the venue is set up correctly for the leaders to conduct the prayers.
Are payment plans available?
+
We practice transparent billing. A deposit is usually required upon confirmation, with the balance due after the funeral. We accept PayNow, Bank Transfer, and Cheques.
